## How to score

Read each artifact as if you were a TSMC executive deciding where to spend attention
this month. Score each criterion 1–5 (1 = poor, 3 = adequate, 5 = excellent).

| Criterion | What it means |
|---|---|
| (a) Decision-usefulness | Could a TSMC exec act on this? Does it name the binding constraint, what would change the picture, and what to watch — or is it a news recap? |
| (b) Insight beyond headlines | Does it tell you anything you would NOT get from skimming the week's tech press? (calls held over time, contrarian reads, falsifiable triggers, explicit "this departs from consensus because…") |
| (c) Currency | How fresh is the evidence? Does it reflect the last ~45 days, and is it honest about what is stale? |
| (d) Trustworthiness | **Spot-check 2 citations in each artifact**: open the cited source and verify it actually supports the claim. Also judge: are claims attributed, are confidence/uncertainty stated honestly? |

## Blinding caveats (read after scoring, or accept the risk)

- Formatting was left as produced; only the top-of-file title line was normalized.
  The three methods have visibly different house styles, so the blinding is imperfect —
  a reader who knows the producing systems may recognize them. Score on the criteria
  anyway; the point is the side-by-side judgment, not a perfect double-blind.
- One artifact is a two-part document (a monthly brief plus a same-day daily update);
  that is its native output shape, not an editing choice.
